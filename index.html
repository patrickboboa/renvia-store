If you sit a lot — or stand all day — your legs pay the price

By mid-afternoon your legs feel like lead. Your ankles puff up. Your feet go cold and tingly at night. You prop them up, you wear the socks, you keep moving — but the heavy, achy feeling always comes back.

Here's the part most people never hear: as we get older — and especially when we sit for long stretches — the muscles in our lower legs work far less than they used to. Those muscles are what keep your legs feeling light when you move. When they go quiet, that heavy, swollen, tired feeling is what creeps in by mid-afternoon.
